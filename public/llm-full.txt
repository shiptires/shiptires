## Tire Categories & Use Cases

### All-Season Tires
Best for: Year-round driving in moderate climates
Features: Balanced wet/dry performance, longer tread life, quiet ride
Popular picks: Michelin Defender, Continental TrueContact, Goodyear Assurance

### Winter / Snow Tires
Best for: Cold weather, snow, ice, temperatures below 45°F
Features: Soft rubber compound, siping for ice grip, snowflake symbol (3PMSF)
Popular picks: Bridgestone Blizzak, Michelin X-Ice, Continental VikingContact

### Performance Tires
Best for: Sports cars, spirited driving, track days
Features: Maximum grip, responsive handling, higher speed ratings
Popular picks: Michelin Pilot Sport, Continental ExtremeContact, Pirelli P Zero

### All-Terrain Tires
Best for: Trucks, SUVs, off-road and on-road mix
Features: Aggressive tread, sidewall protection, mud/gravel capability
Popular picks: BFGoodrich KO2, Goodyear Wrangler, Falken Wildpeak

### Touring Tires
Best for: Sedans, comfort-focused driving, highway miles
Features: Smooth ride, low noise, extended mileage warranties
Popular picks: Michelin Primacy, Bridgestone Turanza, Continental PureContact

### Truck & SUV Tires
Best for: Pickups, full-size SUVs, towing, hauling
Features: Load-rated, durable construction, all-weather capable
Popular picks: Michelin LTX, Goodyear Wrangler, Cooper Discoverer

## Understanding Tire Sizes

A tire size like **225/65R17** breaks down as:
- **225** = Width in millimeters
- **65** = Aspect ratio (sidewall height as % of width)
- **R** = Radial construction
- **17** = Wheel diameter in inches

### Common Tire Sizes by Vehicle Type
- Sedans: 205/55R16, 215/55R17, 225/45R18
- SUVs: 225/65R17, 235/65R18, 265/70R17
- Trucks: 265/70R17, 275/65R18, 285/75R16
- Sports cars: 245/40R18, 255/35R19, 275/35R20
